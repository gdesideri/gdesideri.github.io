I am a doctoral student in the [ELLIS](https://ellis.eu/) Doctoral Program, based at the [University of Technology Nuremberg](https://utn.de), where I work on **continual reinforcement learning** under the supervision of [Claire Vernade](https://cvernade.github.io/) and [Marlos C. Machado](https://webdocs.cs.ualberta.ca/~machado/).

My research centers on building agents that keep learning over their lifetime — adapting to new tasks and environments without forgetting what they already know. More broadly, I am interested in reinforcement learning, online learning, and their applications to real-world decision-making problems.

I hold a Master of Science in Computer Engineering (Artificial Intelligence) from [Politecnico di Milano](https://www.polimi.it/), where my thesis explored a reinforcement learning approach for health-behavioural recommendations to reduce cancer risk. Before starting my PhD, I worked as a machine learning intern at [ML Cube](https://www.mlcube.com/), was a summer researcher at the University of Science and Technology of China, and spent two years as a backend software engineer.
